## Rules

PIXTEL follows standard 8-ball pool rules:

### The Balls

- **Cue ball** (white) — the ball you hit with your cue
- **Solids** (1-7) — one player's group
- **Stripes** (9-15) — the other player's group
- **8-ball** (black) — the winning ball

### Game Flow

1. **Break** — The first player shoots to break the rack
2. **Group assignment** — The first ball potted after the break determines each player's group (solids or stripes)
3. **Turns** — Players alternate turns, trying to pot their assigned balls
4. **8-ball** — Once all your group balls are potted, pot the 8-ball to win

### Fouls

A foul occurs when:
- You pot the cue ball (scratch)
- You don't hit any ball
- Your turn timer expires

After a foul, the opponent gets **ball-in-hand** — they can place the cue ball anywhere on the table before their shot.

### Winning

- Pot all 7 of your group balls, then pot the **8-ball**
- If your opponent leaves the game, you win automatically

### Losing

- Potting the 8-ball before clearing all your group balls results in a loss
- Leaving the table during a game forfeits the match

## Controls

### Aiming

- **Click and drag** from the cue ball to set the shot direction
- A guide line shows the predicted path
- Adjust the angle by moving your mouse/finger

### Power

- The **distance** of your drag determines the shot power
- Short drag = soft shot, long drag = powerful shot
- A power indicator shows the current force level

### Ball-in-Hand

When your opponent fouls:
- A "ball-in-hand" indicator appears
- **Click anywhere** on the table to place the cue ball
- Then take your shot from that position

## Cue Customization

PIXTEL features a **cue rack system** with multiple pool cue designs:

- Open the cue rack before or during a game
- Select from your owned cues
- The default cue is free; premium cues can be purchased from the shop
- Your cue selection is saved per account

## Physics

The pool simulation uses realistic physics:

- Ball-to-ball collisions with proper deflection
- Cushion bounces with energy loss
- Pocket detection with approach zones
- Ball sinking animation (balls glide and shrink into pockets)
- Corner and side pockets have different sizes (side pockets are smaller, as in real pool)

## Pot Distribution

- Both players bet the same amount, forming a shared pot
- The winner takes the entire pot
- If a player disconnects, the remaining player wins by default

## Tips

- **Plan your shots** — think about where the cue ball will end up after the shot
- **Use soft shots** for precise positioning
- **Don't rush the 8-ball** — clear a path first
- **Ball-in-hand is powerful** — use fouls to your advantage by placing the cue ball in the best position
- **Customize your cue** — different cues don't change gameplay but add personal style
